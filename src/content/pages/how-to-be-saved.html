How to Be Saved

Dear Friend, You Can Be Sure of Eternal Life!

God wants you to be 100% certain that when you die you will go to Heaven. What must you know from the Bible to be 100% sure?

1

You Must Realize That You Are a Sinner

"For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God:" Romans 3:23

As a sinner, you will pay the price of sin.

"For the wages of sin is death" - Romans 6:23a

"But the fearful, and unbelieving, and the abominable, and murderers, and whoremongers, and sorcerers, and idolaters, and all liars, shall have their part in the lake which burneth with fire and brimstone: which is the second death." Revelation. 21:8

2

Christ Has Paid the Price for You

"But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us." - Romans 5:8

Repent, turn from your sin and accept Jesus Christ as your personal Saviour. Believe that He died, buried and rose again for your salvation.

"That if thou shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus, and shalt believe in thine heart that God hath raised him from the dead, thou shalt be saved." - Romans 10:9

3

Believe and Receive Christ

If you believe with all your heart these promises, this short pattern of prayer will guide you to talk to God, believing Jesus as your Lord and Saviour. You can do this NOW.

Dear God, I admit that I am a sinner. There is nothing I can do to earn my way to Heaven. Please forgive me of my sins. I believe that your Son, the Lord Jesus Christ, died, buried and rose again to pay for my sins. I now trust Him as my personal Lord and Saviour. I ask Him to come to my life. In Jesus' name, Amen.

4

God's Promises to You

If you pray that prayer, or a prayer like that and you believe with sincerity of heart on what Jesus did on the cross for you, read what God promised…

"But as many as received him, to them gave he power to become the sons of God, even to them that believe on his name:" - John 1:12

"For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life." - John 3:16

"For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved." - Romans 10:13

5

You Have Eternal Life Now

Now you have eternal life and new destination.

"…but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord." - Romans 6:23b

"Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new." - 2 Corinthians 5:17

"For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: Not of works, lest any man should boast." - Ephesians 2:8-9

FAQ's

Common Questions About Salvation

  • No, baptism is not required for salvation. Salvation comes by faith alone in Jesus Christ. Ephesians 2:8-9 clearly states, "For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: Not of works, lest any man should boast." Baptism is an act of obedience that follows salvation, but it is not part of salvation itself. The thief on the cross was saved by faith without being baptized (Luke 23:42-43).

  • No, once you are truly saved, you cannot lose your salvation. Jesus said in John 10:28-29, "And I give unto them eternal life; and they shall never perish, neither shall any man pluck them out of my hand." God's gift of eternal life is just that—eternal. Romans 8:38-39 assures us that nothing can separate us from God's love. True believers are secure in Christ because salvation is God's work, not ours.

  • Repentance means a change of mind about sin and about Christ. It's turning from your sin and turning to Jesus Christ as your Saviour. True repentance involves recognizing you are a sinner in need of a Saviour and believing that Jesus Christ died for your sins and rose again. It's not just feeling sorry for sin, but trusting Christ alone for salvation rather than trusting in your own good works or anything else.

  • You can know for certain because God's Word promises it. 1 John 5:13 says, "These things have I written unto you that believe on the name of the Son of God; that ye may know that ye have eternal life." If you have believed in Jesus Christ as your personal Saviour, trusting that He died for your sins and rose again, then you have eternal life. Your assurance is based on God's promise, not on your feelings or performance.

  • After receiving Christ as your Saviour, you should follow Him in believer's baptism by immersion, join a Bible-believing local church, read and study God's Word daily, pray regularly, and tell others about Jesus Christ. Growth in your Christian life comes through fellowship with other believers, obedience to God's Word, and serving the Lord. Contact us so we can help you take your next steps in following Christ.
